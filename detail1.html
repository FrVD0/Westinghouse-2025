Ballet in Earnest

By Yim Tan Wong

In Atlanta, there were so many ways to dance
No time for study or sleep
No farmer in the dell, no cheese standing alone.
At the Masquerade, Wednesdays were industrial,
Thursdays 80â€™s night, and the weekends ecstasy
Fueled raves: Chemical Brothers, Prodigy, and always
Kazell the opener. Sasha at the Warehouse,
Digweed too, and Sven Vath. Faith lost in everything
But the synergy of pills, strobe lights, and a DJ
Stomping away with us like a puppeteer his creations.
Our feet were on the dance floor,
But we felt lifted, aloft in the air, the music more
Our heart beats than our own hearts.
The club more life than life could offer
In the daylight, on the outside, on the sober.
Then, at some point, you have to go adult,
Slow down, work, take care of aging parents,
Pay the bills you racked up travelling around
Seeking this high from New York to London.
Soon, home is enough, and you are no longer
A wormy thing, you are a feathery light
Glowing mango, raspberry, pink lemonade
Dancing sprite in the kitchen most the time machine.
