Under the Hanging Lanterns

By Poet

They gathered where the cypress leans and stars begin to show, beneath the glow of paper lamps and embers burning low. The table held what hands had made —a loaf, a peach, a stew— and stories passed from mouth to mouth like something old made new. A boy recited verses learned beneath a quilted moon, while elders tapped their feet in time to fiddle, pipe, and tune. No book was kept, no names were carved, no portrait hung in frame— but every dish and dance that night preserved the family name.
