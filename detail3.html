Horizon and Gaze

Blue Not Blue Ballet

Yim Tan Wong

for Mr. Jon Batiste

When Caroline was little, instead of saying
“butterfly”, she would say “buggerfly”
It’s okay Maddy spells Alina's name “Aliena”
She’s the one who was kicked out of ballerina school
For distracting the other children who followed
Her instructions instead of the teacher’s
When Jon Batiste sees he sees beautiful,
He sees joy, life, he sees patent leather gold-
Trimmed shoes that laser beam their shine
Throughout the Boston Opera House
As he mystifies across the stage, he sees
you don’t only have to play the piano keys,
you can also play the strings under the piano’s lid
When we chanted the bridge to his “Butterfly”
The wings of the stage dissolved, the ceiling
Turned into the York cathedral I once sat in
To hear the choir boys practice their angelic tones
We were no longer skin and blood and bone
No longer atoms and elements aging and dying
We were star stuff, sound simply, complex
Hearts beating out a Sacred sound, a tapestry
So supreme, all of us buggers, flying home
