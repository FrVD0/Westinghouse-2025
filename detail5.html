Untitled

By Poet

The windows flicker—codes or dreams? Each streetlight hums in broken beams. A figure waits, coat to the wind, no past behind, no plan to mend. The buildings buzz with restless glow, like insects trapped in glass below. Reflections smear across the street— the city's breath beneath your feet. A pink sign blinks, Desire/Repair, but no one’s buying hope up there. A siren yawns, the echo fades, it’s swallowed whole by neon shades. The rooftop knows the name you dropped, the vow you broke, the time you stopped. But still you stand, above the mess, your silhouette in slight regress. The city sings in static code, and waits for you to crack the mode.
